# Tolerance And Fit Analysis

Status: open-question analysis. No tolerances, dimensions, or fit values are
specified here.

## Fit Philosophy

The octobass mechanism is dominated by alignment and lost motion. Tolerances
cannot be assigned until the single-string rig and ergonomic mockup identify
real loads, deflections, and playable clearances. Every item below is an open
question, not a released fit.

## Critical Open Questions

### String To Stop Contact

- How close must the stop head land to the measured string centerline to avoid
  side loading?
- What contact face shape avoids string damage while still making a clean stop?
- How much rebound or follow-through is acceptable after the stop contacts?
- Should the stop reference the string directly, a backing surface, or a guided
  tangent rail?

### Linkage Motion

- How much input travel can the player comfortably control by hand?
- How much pedal travel is safe without disturbing bow posture?
- How much backlash can exist before pitch onset becomes unreliable?
- Where should adjusters live so service does not require disassembling the
  body?

### Bow Clearance

- What swept volume does the bow need around each string?
- Which stops or linkages risk entering the bow path?
- Should control hardware sit behind the string plane, beside it, or on an
  independent mechanism frame?

### Structural Fit

- Which datum controls the stop mechanism: bridge line, nut/contact line,
  string centerline, body centerline, or mechanism frame?
- How much frame movement occurs when a stop is actuated?
- Does the mechanism frame need to be independent from the resonant body?
- How will the instrument stay stable during foot-pedal actuation?

### Service Fit

- Can a stop pad, cable, rod, spring, or bushing be replaced without removing
  strings?
- Can stops be adjusted with the instrument upright and safely supported?
- What access panels are required before the body outline is finalized?

## Evidence Required Before Assigning Tolerances

- measured stop-contact repeatability;
- measured linkage lost motion;
- measured frame deflection under actuation;
- bow-clearance mockup observations;
- service-access mockup observations;
- expert review of any final datum scheme.
