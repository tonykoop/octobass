# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished concept octobass as a real physical object: a monumental three-string bowed bass towering far above human height, its broad varnished wooden body, massive neck, wide bridge, and long tailpiece reading as a hugely scaled-up upright double bass. Its defining feature is the mechanical string-stopping console placed beside the player's standing position, a bank of large organ-stop-like hand levers and foot pedals linked by slender steel rods, cables, and bell cranks up the neck to padded clamp and tangent stops that press against the three oversized strings at selected positions, so the player can bow the strings while the levers and pedals do the stopping. Deep varnished spruce-and-maple-toned wood, dark fingerboard, brass and steel linkage hardware, clean prototype finish; realistic scale cues from a low standing platform and floor stand with the human-scaled controls dwarfed by the body. Clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic rehearsal-room scene of the giant octobass played by a standing musician, one hand drawing a long bow across the low strings while the other works the lever bank and a foot rests near a pedal control. The enormous body rises above the player toward exposed beams, exposed linkages and remote stops visible but safely routed, with chairs, music stands, a workbench, and hand tools softly out of focus for scale and a making-and-rehearsal story. Natural side light from tall windows, dust in the air, shallow depth of field keeping the console and bowing hands sharp, realistic posture, concept visualization only, no staged fantasy machinery, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the octobass stop-action mechanism: a padded clamp or hard tangent stop approaching one oversized wound bass string at a measured point on the neck, a small bell-crank linkage with adjustable nuts and a return spring, the backing reference surface, and the bridge and string path blurred behind. Show the wound-string texture, wear-safe contact material, brushed steel rods, serviceable fasteners, the join between varnished wood and machined hardware, and clear bowing clearance. Raking light to bring out material textures, shallow depth of field, high detail, no measurements, no text labels.

## Negative / Avoid

No text/logos/watermarks/fake maker marks, no dimension callouts or measurement lines, no impossible lever geometry or free-floating mechanisms, no guitar-sized proportions, no extra strings beyond the three-string concept, no unsafe pinch-point emphasis; anatomically plausible hands and realistic body proportions if a player is shown.
